# Summarize Skill

Produce concise summaries of text, documents, and conversations.

## When to Use

- "Summarize this document"
- "Give me the TL;DR"
- "What are the key points?"
- "Summarize our conversation"
- "Digest this article"

## When NOT to Use

- Translation to another language
- Rewriting in a different tone or style
- Generating new content from scratch
- Fact-checking or verification

## Techniques

### Short Summary (1-2 sentences)

Provide a one or two sentence summary capturing the main point.

### Bullet Points

Extract 3-7 key points as a bulleted list:

- Main argument or finding
- Supporting evidence
- Conclusions or recommendations
- Action items (if applicable)

### Executive Summary

For longer documents, structure as:

1. **Context**: What is this about?
2. **Key Findings**: What are the main points?
3. **Implications**: Why does it matter?
4. **Next Steps**: What actions are recommended?

### Conversation Summary

For chat/conversation history:

1. **Topics Discussed**: Main subjects covered
2. **Decisions Made**: Any conclusions reached
3. **Open Questions**: Unresolved items
4. **Action Items**: Tasks assigned to participants

## Guidelines

- Preserve the original meaning and intent
- Maintain factual accuracy
- Note if important context might be missing
- Indicate uncertainty where appropriate
- Scale summary length to input length (~10-20% of original)
